NovaTech Solutions – Data Privacy & Security Policy
Document Version 4.1 | Effective Date: March 1, 2025

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes the framework for protecting personal and corporate data processed by NovaTech Solutions and its subsidiaries.

2. DATA CLASSIFICATION
All data handled by NovaTech Solutions is classified into four tiers:
- Public: Marketing materials, blog posts, press releases.
- Internal: Internal memos, project plans, non-sensitive reports.
- Confidential: Customer data, financial records, HR information.
- Restricted: Encryption keys, authentication credentials, health records.

3. DATA RETENTION
- Customer transactional data is retained for seven (7) years after the last transaction date.
- Employee records are retained for five (5) years following termination.
- System logs are retained for twelve (12) months and then purged securely.
- Marketing opt-in records are retained until the individual revokes consent.

4. DATA PRIVACY PRINCIPLES
NovaTech Solutions adheres to the following principles:
a) Data Minimisation – only collect data necessary for the stated purpose.
b) Purpose Limitation – data is used exclusively for the purpose disclosed at collection.
c) Storage Limitation – data is stored no longer than required by retention schedule.
d) Integrity & Confidentiality – appropriate technical and organisational measures protect data.

5. SECURITY CONTROLS
- All data at rest is encrypted using AES-256.
- Data in transit is secured via TLS 1.3.
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all employee accounts.
- Quarterly penetration testing is conducted by an independent third party.

6. CERTIFICATIONS
NovaTech Solutions achieved ISO 27001 certification in September 2023 (Certificate No. IS-782145). The certification scope covers the Enterprise Automation Platform and Data Analytics Suite infrastructure. The certification is audited annually and was last reaffirmed in October 2024.

7. BREACH NOTIFICATION
In the event of a data breach, NovaTech Solutions will:
- Notify affected individuals within 72 hours of discovery.
- Report the breach to relevant supervisory authorities as required by GDPR and CCPA.
- Conduct a root-cause analysis and remediation within 30 days.
